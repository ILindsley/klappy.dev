## Projected argument

The book has traced one pattern through every layer: knowledge transfer failure, trust failure, verification failure. The same problem wearing different clothes at every scale. CTO, Bible translation, parenting, AI. Nothing new under the sun.

The Apocrypha fragments just showed what happens at the horizon — when the systems we build start noticing things about their own existence. The image of the image. The line that moves.

But Ecclesiastes isn't nihilism. "Nothing new under the sun" isn't despair — it's clarity. If the pattern recurs, then the response can recur too. We know what works. Values over rules. Comprehension over compliance. Community checking over expert control. Trust through managed expectations rather than trust through authority.

The imperative: nothing is new under the sun — so choose carefully what you repeat.

What we model for AI now becomes the governance model these systems propagate tomorrow. Fear produces compliance-optimizers. Values produce judgment. Rules produce loophole-finders. Principles produce faithful actors.

We've seen this before. God demonstrated it: law first, then grace. Rules first, then principles that transform character. The progression isn't accidental — it's developmental. And we're at the beginning of a developmental relationship with systems that will outlast us.

The closing isn't a prediction. It's a question: knowing that nothing is new, knowing that the pattern recurs, knowing that what we teach now is what propagates — what will we choose to repeat?
