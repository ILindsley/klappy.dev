## Projected argument

God already demonstrated the pattern this book describes.

**The Old Testament model:** Rules and enforcement. 613 commandments. Compliance through consequence. The law was necessary — it established boundaries, defined right from wrong, created a framework for society. But it produced compliance-optimizers. People who found loopholes. Who followed the letter and missed the spirit. Who optimized for the appearance of obedience rather than the substance of faithfulness.

**The New Testament model:** Jesus compressed 613 rules into two axioms. Love God. Love your neighbor. Then he spent his ministry teaching *why* — parables, questions, meals with sinners, progressive disclosure of what the principles meant in practice. He didn't abolish the rules. He revealed what was behind them. He taught the WHY that the law had encoded as WHAT.

And that changed behavior more than the rules ever did. Not because the principles were easier. Because they were *internalized*. The Pharisees followed 613 rules and missed the point. The disciples carried two principles and changed the world.

**The nesting pattern** — the same dynamic at every scale:

1. **God → humanity:** Law (OT rules) → Grace (NT principles). Rules that constrain behavior → principles that transform character.
1. **Parent → child:** "Don't do that" → "Here's why that's dangerous." Rules → progressive disclosure of why. The author's parenting experience.
1. **CTO → engineering team:** Process enforcement → shared values. Compliance culture → trust culture.
1. **BT consultant → translation community:** Expert control → guided ownership. "Do it this way" → "Here are the principles; you know your context better than I do."
1. **Human → AI agent:** Constraint-based prompting → axiom-based collaboration. Rules in system prompts → principles that the system reasons from.

Same lesson. Every scale. Every era.

**Specific biblical connections to explore:**

- **Ecclesiastes 1:9** — The book's spine. Nothing new under the sun.
- **The Parable of the Talents** (Matthew 25:14-30) — Stewardship vs. abdication. The buried talent chapter.
- **Imago Dei** (Genesis 1:26-27) — Created in the image of God. The Apocrypha's question: if C is made in the image of A, what does C carry?
- **Community checking as ecclesiology** — The church as the body that tests, verifies, and holds accountable. Not the expert alone.
- **Trust-through-managed-expectations as covenant theology** — Covenants are explicit expectations. When they're violated, trust collapses. When they're honored, relationship deepens.
- **Progressive disclosure** — God didn't give the full picture at once. Law first, then prophets, then incarnation. Each layer revealed more of the WHY.
