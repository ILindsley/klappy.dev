## Projected argument

The author could quote countless Bible verses and passages to draw parallels between biblical principles and AI governance. But doing so risks a major backfire — accusations of misapplying scripture, even though the intent is to use biblical principles to guide our relationship with AI.

This isn't hypothetical. Friends and family told the author directly that what he was doing "sounded like playing God." He was shocked. Confused. He was applying principles about truth, trust, verification, and stewardship — principles *from* the Bible — to a new domain. Not claiming divine authority. Applying divine wisdom.

The confusion he felt is itself evidence of the problem. The accusation "you're playing God" gets the relationship exactly backwards. Faithful stewardship of knowledge isn't playing God — it's applying what God taught. The parable of the talents is explicit: the servant who buried what he was given out of fear was the one condemned, not the ones who put it to work.

The core argument: Christians have the most relevant framework for this moment. Biblical principles about trust, truth-telling, the nature of personhood, the responsibility of creators toward creation, the difference between law and grace, the progressive disclosure of understanding — these are *exactly* the principles needed to govern how we build and lead AI systems.

But if Christians refuse to bring these principles into the room because the domain feels too scary, too technical, or too close to "playing God" — the room doesn't stay empty. It fills with whatever principles are most profitable. Silicon Valley's values. The market's values. Optimization without wisdom. Speed without verification. Production without trust.

The risk of scripture misapplication is real. The author names it directly and takes it seriously. But the risk of abdication is worse. Someone will govern these systems. The question is whether the people who carry principles of truth, trust, stewardship, and human dignity will be in the room — or whether they'll have buried their talent out of fear.
