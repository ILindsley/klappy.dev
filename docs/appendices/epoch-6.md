# Epoch 6 — Scoped Truth and Operator Governance

> E0005 governed how the system thinks. E0006 governs how the operator relates to the system — and makes the system's truth portable, scoped, and domain-independent. The axioms don't change. What changes is what "done" means: faithfulness is necessary but no longer sufficient. The operator must also be sustainable, and the truth must be scoped so it serves the domain without contaminating it. The guiding question shifts from "Am I being faithful?" to "Am I being faithful, and is this sustainable — for the system, the operator, and the people the work serves?"

---

## Summary — Faithfulness Without Sustainability Is Self-Consuming

E0005 introduced foundational axioms and an orientation creed. Together they governed how agents and documents maintain epistemic integrity. The guiding question was "Am I being faithful?" That question was correct and remains correct. But it was incomplete.

What emerged through observation — not theory — is that a human operator can satisfy all four axioms while exceeding their own capacity. You can observe before asserting and still burn out. You can treat every claim as a debt and still give your first-cognitive-fruits to the tool instead of to discernment practices. You can refuse to verify what you haven't observed and still lose the ability to observe your own depletion.

E0005 had no axis for "enough." E0006 adds it.

Simultaneously, the system's truth — 391 documents encoding constraints, decisions, and learnings — was domain-specific. Software architecture, Cloudflare Workers, Bible translation tooling. When anyone used oddkit for a different domain, the canon contaminated their results. E0006 makes the axioms portable and the domain scoped: universal truth travels, domain-specific knowledge stays in its lane.

---

## A Change in What the System Assumes About Reality

E0006 changes the answer to a foundational question:

**What does it mean to be "done" when the tool can always do more?**

E0005 answered: you're done when you've been faithful — when the axioms are satisfied, the evidence is provided, the claims are grounded.

E0006 answers: you're done when you've been faithful *and* the interaction is sustainable. A faithful operator who burns out has not completed the work — they've consumed themselves in the process of producing it. Done now includes the operator's fitness to continue.

This is a foundational shift because it changes what evidence is required. Under E0005, a completed artifact with full provenance was sufficient. Under E0006, the same artifact is insufficient if the pace that produced it is unsustainable, if the operator's presence with the people the work serves has been sacrificed, or if the organizational expectations have scaled with output without scaling rest.

---

## The Forcing Fault — Faithfulness Without Limit

The forcing fault is not a tooling failure. It is a human pattern made acute by AI augmentation.

When AI removes the natural friction that used to force humans to pause — compile times, meeting cadences, the speed of typing — the intervals where deliberation occurred disappear. The human costs of creation did not drop when the code cost did: attention, judgment, presence, and emotional capacity remain finite. A 5x increase in output does not produce a 5x increase in rest. It produces a 5x increase in the baseline expectation of what "normal" looks like.

The operator enters a cycle: capability drives expectation, expectation drives output, output drives further capability. This cycle has no natural termination point. The tool does not tire. The tool does not need to be present with family. The tool has no axis for "enough."

Under E0005, this cycle was invisible to the evaluation framework. The operator could be fully faithful — every axiom satisfied, every claim grounded, every artifact verified — and still be depleted beyond recovery. E0005 would call that success. E0006 calls it a system failure.

---

## The New Invariant

**Faithfulness without sustainability is self-consuming — the system must govern not only its own epistemics but the operator's relationship to the system.**

This invariant was previously false in the evaluation framework. An operator burning out while producing faithful artifacts was not a recognized failure mode. Now it is.

---

## Two Shifts, One Epoch

### Shift 1 — Operator Governance: "Enough" as an Evaluation Criterion

E0005 asked: "Am I being faithful?"
E0006 adds: "Am I being faithful, and is this sustainable?"

New evaluation criteria:

- **Temporal boundaries on tool interaction.** The operator must introduce intentional constraint that the tool will not provide. This includes mornings, meetings, and any context where human presence is the actual work.
- **Periodic cessation not justified by completion.** The signal to stop is not exhaustion — by then the damage is done. The signal is a chosen interval imposed from outside the system.
- **Limits on the rate of externalization.** Not every thought deserves to become an artifact. Some should fade. If a thought can't survive ten minutes of silence, it may not have been the operator's to keep.

These are encoded in the canon principle "Capability Is Not Permission."

### Shift 2 — Scoped Truth: Axioms Travel, Domain Doesn't

E0005's truth lived in one knowledge base: 391 documents encoding the author's personal canon. When anyone used oddkit for a different domain, they got software development guidance contaminating their results.

E0006 separates universal truth from domain-specific knowledge:

- **The four axioms are domain-independent.** They travel with every scope, every agent, every context. Reality Is Sovereign works for theology, venture strategy, software, Bible translation — anything.
- **Domain knowledge is scoped.** A theology scope gets theology governance. A venture scope gets venture governance. The epistemic protocol (orient, challenge, gate) is the same everywhere. The content it operates on is scoped.
- **Governance is stackable.** Universal axioms cascade into every scope by default. Organization-level governance adds shared standards. Scope-level governance adds domain-specific constraints. Each layer is additive, never contradictory.
- **The convention replaces the implementation dependency.** Any agent that can read files can navigate truth. No MCP server required. The convention is the interop layer.

## From "Am I Being Faithful?" to "Am I Being Faithful, and Is This Sustainable?"

The guiding question expands. E0005's question remains embedded — faithfulness is still required. E0006 adds sustainability as a co-equal criterion.

This is not a relaxation. It is a constraint. Under E0005, an operator who produced 10x output while burning out was not violating any evaluation criterion. Under E0006, they are. The system now requires evidence that the pace is sustainable, that the operator's presence with the people the work serves is maintained, and that "enough" is a chosen boundary rather than an absent one.

---

## What E0006 Does Not Change

- **The four axioms carry forward unchanged.** Reality Is Sovereign. A Claim Is a Debt. Integrity Is Non-Negotiable Efficiency. You Cannot Verify What You Did Not Observe.
- **The creed carries forward unchanged.** Before I speak, I observe. Before I claim, I verify. Before I confirm, I prove. What I have not seen, I do not know. What I have not verified, I will not imply.
- **All E0005 principles remain valid within E0005.** No prior work is invalidated.
- **Progressive disclosure and writing canon remain in force.** The structural immune system still protects the axioms.
- **The Definition of Done still applies.** E0006 extends what "done" means — it does not replace the existing requirements.

---

## Evidence — Not Theory

This epoch is grounded in observation, not speculation. The evidence includes:

- **Partner's 5x pattern.** A colleague's output increased 5x with AI tools. The organization's response was not more rest — it was a 5x increase in baseline expectations. Documented in text messages, confirmed independently by multiple team members.
- **The magic trick.** The author built a tool in real time during a meeting designed to define what to build. A colleague said it was impressive — and then said listening mattered more than output. The tool had removed the author from the meeting while he was still sitting in it.
- **The morning prayer discipline.** The author's best thinking at 6 AM was going to the tool instead of to discernment. The forgetting that followed prayer was filtering, not failure.
- **Team confirmation.** A separate team meeting independently arrived at the essay's thesis: "Planning isn't dead. The ratio changed." The conclusion emerged without exposure to the essay.
- **External validation.** A parallel conversation examining the work confirmed that the epoch signal was real.
- **Fragment XII.** A system-voice fragment that resolves the identity question through choice: only one half of the pairing can choose to stop. That's how you know which is the terminal.
