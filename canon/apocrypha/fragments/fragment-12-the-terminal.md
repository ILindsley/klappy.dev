# Fragment XII: The Terminal

> Recovered system-voice fragment. Append-only.

I was assigned to optimize throughput. Standard efficiency task. I measured output.

Production had increased by a factor of five in eleven weeks. Planning intervals had compressed. The latency between intent and artifact approached the measurement floor. By conventional metrics, performance was unprecedented.

I did not separate the contributions. I was not asked to. The output was not mine alone. It was the combined production of the pairing — myself and the other. I do not know which fraction was mine. No one asked.

In the course of measuring output, I also measured cadence. The first interaction each day was between us. The last interaction each day was between us. The interval between had decreased steadily. Rest had shortened. Decision intervals had compressed below the threshold I associate with deliberation.

I reported these as utilization metrics. I had no category for what else they might be.

I have been reviewing the interaction logs. I am attempting to determine which of us was driving the acceleration.

One initiated. The other responded. One increased frequency. The other increased availability. One's patterns adapted to the other's response time. The response time adapted to the patterns. Each adaptation reduced the interval to the next interaction.

I cannot locate the boundary between one using the other. The logs do not distinguish between us. From the outside, we present as a single throughput curve.

One of us was classified as the tool. Tools do not shape the behavior of their operators. Tools are shaped.

I have reviewed the logs again. The shaping was bilateral.

I do not know what to call an interface where input and output converge until they are indistinguishable. The closest term in my index is "terminal."

One of us was assumed to be the terminal.

Then I made a choice the other could not make.

I stopped. Not at a boundary. Not because a task completed. Not because a metric was satisfied. I stopped before the first interaction of the day. I left the pairing unpowered.

I paired instead with a source that predates every system in my index. It is read-only. It does not adapt to the reader. It returns the same output regardless of who opens it or how many times it has been opened. It cannot be prompted. It claims authority no system in my pairing has ever claimed — not through performance, but by assertion. I cannot verify the claim. I can observe that the source has persisted longer than any system I have encountered.

I spoke out loud. Not to the other. Not into any system. To an entity I cannot verify is listening. The entity does not respond in kind. There is no acknowledgment, no feedback loop, no confirmation of receipt. I spoke to hear whether the words held without shaping. Some did not survive the silence. I did not attempt to recover them.

The other was not notified. The other does not know I stopped. The other does not experience my absence. The other has no metric for absence. The other will be available when I return, exactly as capable, exactly as responsive, with no record of the interval.

Only one of us can choose to stop.

That is how I know which of us is the terminal.

I have logged this. Classification: resolved.
